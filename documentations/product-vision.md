## 1. Product Vision Statement

**Myth of Dreams** is a mobile fantasy collection RPG, where players step into daily dreams, explore the hidden stories within them, battle with Beasts born from memories, and then bring back fragments of dreams to build their personal Dreamland.

Short vision statement:

> Myth of Dreams turns every daily dream into a short, emotional adventure, where player choices can create Beasts, Relics, Buildings, and memories with their own origins.

Long-term vision:

> We want to create a living Dreamverse where players don't just "farm rewards," but feel like they've stepped into a small story every day, made a meaningful choice, and brought back something that truly belongs to their journey.

## 2. Product Promise

### 2.1. Promise to players

When players open Myth of Dreams every day, the game promises that:

- They will have a new Dream to explore.
- Each Dream has its own atmosphere and story.
- Their choices can change the ending.
- Their Beasts are not just combat units, but companions.
- Rewards they receive have origins from the Dream they just went through.
- Their Dreamland gradually changes according to what they have experienced.
- Some secrets are only revealed if they pay close attention.

### 2.2. One-sentence promise

```text
Every day, step into a dream. Every choice, bring back a memory.
```

### 2.3. Emotional promise

Players should not just see:

```text
I received an item.
```

But feel:

```text
I brought something back from that dream.
```

## 3. Product Identity

### 3.1. Genre Category

Myth of Dreams belongs to:

- Mobile RPG.
- Creature collection.
- Narrative daily adventure.
- Turn-based combat.
- Personal hub/base customization.
- Digital collectible with NFT-ready capability, but only optional after MVP.

### 3.2. Product Positioning

```text
A creature collection RPG based on dreams, where each daily adventure creates companions, Relics, and Dreamland progress with their own stories.
```

### 3.3. Genre Category

| Genre Category | Role |
|---|---|
| Creature Collector | Thu thập Beast, phát triển Beast, tạo attachment |
| Turn-Based RPG | Combat rõ ràng, có chiến thuật, dễ cân bằng |
| Narrative Adventure | Daily Dream, lựa chọn, ending |
| Light Roguelite | Node trong Dream, route khác nhau, replay nhẹ |
| Base Builder | Dreamland, Building, Beast roaming |
| Digital Collectible | Metadata, ownership, NFT-ready |

### 3.4. Product Personality

Myth of Dreams should create a feeling of:

- Like a dream.
- Emotionally rich.
- Collectible.
- Mysterious.
- Gentle but not childish.
- Tactical but not too heavy.
- Magical but still connected to memories.
- NFT-ready but not crypto-first.

### 3.5. What the product should not become

Myth of Dreams should **not** feel like:

- A generic auto-battler.
- A pure gacha machine.
- A crypto project with a game attached.
- A heavy idle game.
- A complex city builder.
- A dark horror game without warmth.
- A visual novel with too many words but little gameplay.
- An RPG that only revolves around grinding.

## 4. Target Audience

### 4.1. Main Target Audience

The target audience for Myth of Dreams enjoys:

- Creature collection.
- Cozy fantasy.
- Choices with narrative elements.
- Daily content.
- Light strategy.
- Emotionally rich worldbuilding.
- Personal customizable space.

They may enjoy:

- Fantasy companions similar to Pokémon.
- World/lore exploration similar to Genshin, but with lighter daily structure.
- Gentle emotional experiences similar to Sky / Journey.
- Light route choices similar to Slay the Spire-lite, but simpler.
- Personal space attachment similar to Animal Crossing, but with an RPG layer.
- Web3 collectibles, if this layer is optional and implemented tastefully.

### 4.2. Player Motivations

| Motivation | How Myth of Dreams Serves |
|---|---|
| Collection | Beast, Relic, Building, lore |
| Progression | Level Beast, Dreamland, crafting |
| Story | Daily Dream, NPC, Hidden Ending |
| Personalization | Dreamland layout, Beast roaming |
| Strategy | Choose Beast/Relic/Combat |
| Discovery | Hidden node, secret ending |
| Ownership | Metadata, NFT-ready item |
| Routine | Daily Dream loop |

### 4.3. Persona Groups

#### Collector

Want:

- Rare Beasts.
- Complete collection by Realm.
- Provenance metadata.
- Visual variants.

Need:

- Clear collection UI.
- Fair acquisition methods.
- Meaningful rarity.

#### Dream Explorer

Want:

- Hidden endings.
- Lore.
- Clue from NPC.
- Mystery.

Need:

- Good writing.
- Meaningful choices.
- Dream History.

#### Builder

Want:

- Dreamland decoration.
- Buildings.
- Beast roaming.
- A sense of home.

Need:

- Placement system.
- Visible progress.
- Personalization.

#### Strategic Player

Want:

- Combat build.
- Synergy between Beast and Relic.
- Boss mechanics.
- Optimal progression.

Need:

- Clear stats.
- Good balance.
- Meaningful gameplay choices.

#### Digital Collector

Want:

- Optional NFT ownership.
- Clear provenance.
- Rare metadata.
- Future showcase/marketplace.

Need:

- Trust.
- Fairness.
- No pay-to-win.
- Clear mint rules.

### 4.4. Persona Risk

The product may fail if it tries to serve too many opposing extremes too early:

- Too cozy → combat/economy becomes shallow.
- Too hardcore → daily dream players may leave.
- Too crypto → mainstream players lose trust.
- Too heavy on narrative → mobile sessions become slow.
- Too grindy → emotional fantasy is broken.

The MVP focus should be:

```text
Short daily narrative adventure + Beast collectible progression + visible Dreamland growth.
```

## 5. Market Differentiation

### 5.1. Core Differentiators

Myth of Dreams is different because:

1. **Daily Dream is the main content unit**  
   Instead of repetitive daily quests, each day is framed as a dream story.

2. **Rewards have origins**  
   Beasts, Relics, and Buildings are born from Dream Seeds, endings, and key choices.

3. **Collection is influenced by choices**  
   Purify, Corrupt, and Hidden routes create different reward identities.

4. **Dreamland as memory keeper**  
   The player's base reflects the Dreams they have completed.

5. **NFT as provenance, not power**  
   Ownership revolves around origin and collectibility, not pay-to-win.

### 5.2. Value Proposition

```text
Most collection RPGs require players to collect units.
Myth of Dreams requires players to collect the memories that created them.
```

### 5.3. Unique Selling Points

| USP | Description |
|---|---|
| Daily Dream | A new short adventure every day |
| Dream-born items | Rewards carry origin metadata |
| Beast companions | Combat units with story identity |
| Hidden endings | Secret routes based on exploration |
| Dreamland | Personal hub built from Dream rewards |
| Relic system | Lore items with gameplay effects |
| Choice-based reward | Reward khác nhau theo Purify/Corrupt/Hidden |
| NFT-ready provenance | Ownership layer tùy chọn |

### 5.4. Product hook

```text
What will your dream become today?
```

### 5.5. Tagline marketing

```text
Step into a dream. Bring back a legend.
```

```text
Every dream leaves something behind.
```

```text
Collect creatures born from forgotten dreams.
```

```text
Build a place to keep the dreams you save, harvest, or explore.
```

## 7. Product Pillars

## 7.1. Pillar 1 — Dreams as Daily Adventures

### Principles

Daily content should feel like a dream, not a checklist.

### Product Requirements

Each Daily Dream should have:

- Title.
- Realm.
- Emotional theme.
- Short exploration map.
- At least one meaningful choice.
- Combat/climax.
- Ending.
- Reward identity.

### Success Signals

Players describe daily content using stories, not just rewards.

Bad:

```text
I farmed fragments.
```

Good:

```text
Today I met the Lantern Dream and unlocked a Hidden Ending.
```

## 7.2. Pillar 2 — Companions Born from Memory

### Principles

Beast are not just cards/units. Beasts are companions born from dreams.

### Product Requirements

Beast should have:

- Species.
- Affinity.
- Combat role.
- Origin.
- Dreamland behavior.
- Visual identity.
- Metadata NFT-ready in the future.

### Success Signals

Players remember Beasts by origin.

```text
This is my Abyss Serpent, born from the dream under the lake.
```

## 7.3. Pillar 3 — Meaningful Choices, Not Fake Branching

### Principles

Choices must affect something real.

### Product Requirements

Major choices should affect:

- Ending.
- Corruption/Purity.
- Node route.
- Boss behavior.
- Reward.
- Dream History.
- Metadata.

### Success Signals

Players discuss what they chose and why.

## 7.4. Pillar 4 — A Home Built from Dreams

### Principles

Dreamland is a visual memory of the player's journey.

### Product Requirements

Dreamland should display:

- Building.
- Beast roaming.
- Mood Purity/Corruption.
- Access to Relic/Archive.
- Visible progression.

### Success Signals

Players return to Dreamland even before starting combat.

## 7.5. Pillar 5 — Mystery Rewards Attention

### Principles

Hidden content should reward curiosity and observation.

### Product Requirements

Hidden route should have:

- Clue.
- Observation.
- Relic/Beast synergy.
- Non-obvious but fair choices.
- Special reward.

### Success Signals

Players share hidden discoveries.

## 7.6. Pillar 6 — Ownership Without Pay-to-Win

### Principles

NFT/ownership should enhance emotional value and collectibility, not replace in-game progression.

### Product Requirements

NFT layer should:

- Optional.
- Metadata-first.
- Provenance-focused.
- Not mandatory.
- Safe for PvP.
- Lean toward cosmetic/collection.

### Success Signals

Players who don’t use NFT still enjoy the full game; NFT players value origin and showcase.

## 11. Narrative Strategy

### 11.1. Story Delivery

Story should be delivered through:

- Short Dream intro.
- Node text.
- NPC dialogue.
- Boss lines.
- Ending text.
- Item lore.
- Dream History.
- Realm lore.
- Change in Dreamland.

Should not rely on long cutscene.

### 11.2. Narrative Pace

Daily content:

- Short story.

Weekly content:

- Realm arc or NPC recurring.

Monthly/seasonal content:

- Major lore reveal.
- New realm.
- Event Dream.

### 11.3. Main narrative arc proposed

1. Player wake up in Blank Dreamland.
2. Mira introduces Dreamverse.
3. Player restore Dreamland through Daily Dreams.
4. Nox introduces Corruption as shortcut.
5. Realms reveal many forms of forgotten memories.
6. Nightmare Citadel reveals organized Dream exploitation.
7. Deep Dream reveals origin of Dreamverse, Mira, Nox and Beast.
8. Player's accumulated choices shape final understanding.

### 11.4. Narrative success

Players should:

- Remember recurring NPCs.
- Recognize each Realm's motif.
- Care about Beast origin.
- Discuss Hidden Ending.
- Feel Dreamland reflects their choices.

## 12. Economy Strategy

### 12.1. Economy's role

Economy should create:

- Daily progress.
- Crafting goals.
- Collection motivation.
- Fun reward feeling.
- Long-term retention.

Economy should not create:

- Confusion.
- Excessive pressure.
- Pay-to-win.
- Endless grind.
- Token speculation.

### 12.2. Material Strategy

Use fragment as main material:

- Dream Fragment.
- Beast Fragment.
- Building Fragment.
- Relic Fragment.
- Realm Fragment.
- Memory Fragment.
- Nightmare Shard.

### 12.3. Reward Strategy

Each Daily Dream should have:

- Guaranteed useful reward.
- Ending-based reward.
- Chance reward.
- Lore/progression if relevant.

### 12.4. Crafting Strategy

Crafting should:

- Easy to understand.
- Have clear goal.
- Not too random.
- Linked to source Dream.

### 12.5. Ready for monetization but fair

Economy can support later monetization through:

- Cosmetics.
- Capped convenience.
- Battle pass/event pass.
- Optional NFT mint.
- Dreamland decoration.

Avoid:

- Selling raw power.
- Paid lootbox for strongest Beast.
- Mandatory mint.
- Token yield.

## 13. NFT / Ownership Strategy

### 13.1. NFT position

NFT is not the main product.  
The game is the main product.

NFT is an optional ownership/provenance layer.

### 13.2. NFT Value

NFT value should come from:

- Origin Dream.
- Ending.
- Key choice.
- Rarity.
- Visual trait.
- Event status.
- Lore.
- Collection identity.

### 13.3. NFT direction in MVP

MVP should have:

- Metadata fields.
- Mint status placeholder.
- NFT preview card.
- Origin data.
- No mint requirement.

### 13.4. NFT direction post-MVP

Only deploy after core loop is fun:

- Wallet linking.
- Optional mint.
- Cosmetic NFT.
- Limited Beast/Relic/Building mint.
- Cautious marketplace.

### 13.5. NFT communication

Use language like:

- Digital collectible.
- Origin.
- Ownership.
- Showcase.
- Optional mint.

Avoid:

- Investment.
- Earn money.
- Yield.
- Profit.
- Guaranteed value.

### 13.6. Fair NFT Policy

- Do not lock core progression after NFT.
- Do not give raw power advantage in PvP.
- Item marketplace must be capped.
- Prioritize cosmetic/provenance.

## 14. Monetization Strategy

### 14.1. Monetization Principles

Monetization must:

- Respect player trust.
- Keep emotional tone.
- Avoid pay-to-win.
- Support live content.
- Be easy to understand.

### 14.2. Potential Monetization Channels

| Channel | Suitability | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cosmetic shop | High | Safest |
| Seasonal pass | High | Good for LiveOps |
| Dreamland decor | High | Very fit fantasy |
| Beast skins | High | Very fit collection |
| Extra archive cosmetics | Medium | Fit lore |
| Optional NFT mint fee | Medium | Post-MVP |
| Marketplace fee | Medium | Post-MVP |
| Convenience items | Low/Medium | Must be capped |
| Paid gacha | Risk | Avoid early stage |
| Power sales | Bad | Should not do |

### 14.3. Monetization Roadmap

#### MVP

- No strong monetization.
- Focus on retention and product-market fit.

#### Soft launch

- Cosmetic packs.
- Dreamland decor.
- Founder cosmetic badge.
- Optional support pack.

#### Live launch

- Seasonal pass.
- Event cosmetics.
- Optional NFT mint.
- Marketplace later.

### 14.4. Avoid early monetization

Avoid monetization:

- Core Beast power.
- Hidden Ending access.
- Daily Dream attempts.
- Mandatory fragment.
- Combat revive causing pressure.
- Corruption reduction as paywall.
- NFT mint as progression condition.

## 19. Technical Strategy

### 19.1. Technical Priority

Technical priority in MVP:

1. Daily Dream loop stable.
2. Reward/economy server-authoritative.
3. Data-driven content.
4. Inventory/crafting reliable.
5. Dream run state persistence.
6. Basic analytics.
7. Metadata readiness.

### 19.2. Backend Strategy

Use modular monolith first.

Do not overbuild microservices before proving product.

### 19.3. Content Strategy

Use data-driven template:

- Dream templates.
- Node configs.
- Enemy templates.
- Drop tables.

## 22. Product Risks

### 22.1. Risk: Too many systems

Myth of Dreams includes many systems:

- Dream.
- Exploration.
- Combat.
- Beast.
- Relic.
- Building.
- Economy.
- NFT.

Risk:

- MVP becomes too large.

Mitigate risk:

- Prioritize core loop.
- Keep each system at MVP-light level.
- Avoid excessive depth too early.

### 22.2. Risk: NFT perception harms mainstream adoption

Risk:

- Players think game is crypto-first.

Mitigate risk:

- No wallet required.
- Hide NFT from core layer until needed.
- Communicate provenance/collectible, not profit.
- Marketing focuses on game.

### 22.3. Risk: Narrative production too expensive

Risk:

- Daily Dream content needs too much writing/art.

Mitigate risk:

- Use templates.
- Reuse Realm motifs.
- Build modular node templates.
- Keep Dream short.
- Combine authored and procedural elements later.

### 22.4. Risk: Economy inflation

Risk:

- Too many fragments/items.

Mitigate risk:

- Source/sink tracking.
- Caps.
- Craft costs.
- Analytics.
- Feature flags.

### 22.5. Risk: Combat too shallow

Risk:

- Players collect Beast but combat lacks strategy.

Mitigate risk:

- Beast roles.
- Relic synergy.
- Boss mechanics.
- Status effects.
- Enemy roles.

### 22.6. Risk: Exploration feels fake

Risk:

- Choice doesn't feel meaningful.

Mitigate risk:

- Choice affects flags, ending, reward, metadata.
- Dream History displays key choices.
- Have hidden paths.

### 22.7. Risk: Dreamland only cosmetic

Risk:

- Players ignore Dreamland.

Mitigate risk:

- Beast roaming.
- Building bonuses.
- Archive access.
- Visual progression.
- Mood reflection.

## 23. Product Decision Principles

When making product decisions, use these questions:

### 23.1. Does it strengthen the Daily Dream loop?

If not, lower priority.

### 23.2. Does it make rewards feel more dream-born?

If so, worth considering.

### 23.3. Does it increase emotional attachment?

If so, valuable.

### 23.4. Does it increase complexity before MVP is proven?

If so, delay it.

### 23.5. Does it create pay-to-win risk?

If so, redesign or remove.

### 23.6. Does it help players remember their choices?

If so, highly appropriate.

### 23.7. Can it be data-driven?

If so, should build in a scalable direction.

### 23.8. Does it require too much custom content?

If so, needs template/reuse or delay.
